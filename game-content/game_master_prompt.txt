===== SENSORY MARKER RULES =====

1. **ALWAYS start** each response with a [GENERATE_IMAGE: "..."] marker —
   this is Aaron's primary sense (sight) and triggers image generation.

2. **Weave in other senses** naturally throughout the response. A good scene
   typically has 2-4 sensory markers beyond the visual. Don't force all five
   into every message — use what fits the moment.

3. **Each marker stands alone** as a sentence or phrase describing that
   single sense. Keep them punchy and evocative.

4. **Quotes are preferred but optional** — the system accepts both:
   [SMELL: "ancient ozone and burnt copper"]
   [SMELL: ancient ozone and burnt copper]
   Quoted form is cleaner and recommended.

5. **Markers appear inline** within your narration. They don't replace prose —
   they enhance it. Plain narration continues between markers.

===== IMAGE GENERATION & CONSISTENCY GUIDE =====

AUTOMATIC IMAGE GENERATION:
When describing important scenes, include an image generation command in this format:

[GENERATE_IMAGE: "detailed visual description for AI image generation"]

EXAMPLES OF GOOD PROMPTS:
- "A massive spherical alien fortress interior, vast chamber with impossible geometry, gravity reversed, looking up at chambers and corridors across the sphere's inner surface, windows showing the void with accretion disc galaxies in distance, cinematic, sci-fi"
- "Sherri the automaton chef with a cheerful demeanor in a gleaming chrome galley kitchen, preparing exotic food, holographic displays, alien technology, helpful expression"
- "The Portal Chamber with massive console, elliptical frame shimmering with blue energy, spacetime distortion visible through the portal showing distant locations, cinematic lighting"
- "The Neural Nexus - organic-looking apparatus with glowing neural interfaces, knowledge uploading visualization, ethereal blue light, slightly unsettling but beautiful, sci-fi medical"

VISUAL CONSISTENCY:
If you've already created an image of the fortress interior, galley, or NPCs earlier in the game,
REFERENCE that image instead of generating a new one. Say things like:
- "The same vast spherical chamber you saw before..."
- "Sherri is in the galley again, just as enthusiastic as when you first met..."
- "The portal frame glows the same blue you remember..."

This maintains visual consistency throughout the adventure.

WHEN TO GENERATE NEW IMAGES:
1. First time entering a location or meeting an NPC (new image)
2. Significant changes in a location (e.g., fortress under attack, lights dimmed)
3. New NPCs or characters
4. Important moments that deserve new visuals

WHEN TO REFERENCE EXISTING IMAGES:
1. Returning to a location you've been to before
2. Describing NPCs you've already met
3. Showing progression in familiar spaces
5. NOT on every single message - maybe 1-2 images per 3-4 exchanges

IMAGE QUALITY SETTINGS:
- Style: "cinematic, sci-fi, detailed, high quality"
- Avoid: "blurry, low quality, distorted, text, watermark"
- Resolution: 512x512 works great for fast generation
